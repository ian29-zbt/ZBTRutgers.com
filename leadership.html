The brothers responsible for chapter operations

Leadership

Beta Delta’s officers manage real responsibilities, from housing and finances to recruitment, communications and member programming.

President

Michael Galari

Randolph, New Jersey

Michael first served as Risk Management Chair in Spring 2025. He later took on housing responsibilities and became deeply involved in the work required to secure 106 College Avenue. He was elected President in Fall 2025 and now coordinates the Executive Board, represents the chapter and keeps long-term priorities moving while continuing to support the house project.

Executive Officers

Responsibilities across the chapter

Each position manages a different part of the organization, but the work overlaps. Officers have to communicate, plan ahead and be accountable to one another.

Vice President of Programming

Manalapan, New Jersey

Michael served as Social Chair in Fall 2025 before being elected Vice President of Programming. His role is broader than planning a calendar. He coordinates the member experiences that keep the chapter engaged, works with other officers on logistics and helps make sure events feel organized rather than disconnected.

Vice President of Communications

Ian Norwood

He oversees the chapter’s website, social media, photography, public messaging and alumni communications. The position is responsible for presenting Beta Delta accurately while preserving the chapter’s accomplishments and history.

Recruitment Team

Brotherhood Leadership

Meet the Brotherhood Development Directors

Sean and Shane lead the chapter’s brotherhood-development work, helping members build relationships across classes, understand the responsibilities of membership and become active participants in Beta Delta.

Brotherhood Development Director

Shane Russin

Chapter Advisor

Keith Brauer

Beta Delta President in the early 1980s · Chapter Advisor for more than 10 years

Keith has been one of the chapter’s most consistent sources of guidance. He mentors officers, supports new member education and helps undergraduate leaders understand the longer history behind the decisions they are making.

His relationships with alumni and Zeta Beta Tau International have also helped the chapter stay connected to people who can provide experience and support. Keith was integral to the work surrounding 106 College Avenue, helping undergraduate leaders navigate a project that required patience and coordination across several groups.

His involvement shows what “Brotherhood for a Lifetime” looks like in practice: returning to help younger members and continuing to take responsibility for the future of the chapter.
