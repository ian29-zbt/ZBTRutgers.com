Joe Glaser

Founder of Beta Delta

Joe Glaser helped establish the Beta Delta Chapter at Rutgers University in 1948 and served as chapter advisor from the chapter’s founding era in the late 1940s until his death in the 1980s. His leadership established the foundation that generations of brothers continue to build upon.

Joe’s continued presence—sharing meals, attending chapter gatherings and appearing in later composites—shows that his role extended far beyond the founding moment. He is a member of the Beta Delta Hall of Fame.

An early portrait and 10 Union Street

Beta Delta brothers help create the first Rutgers University Dance Marathon, beginning a tradition that continues today.

Beta Delta rechartered

Brothers and alumni celebrate the chapter’s return during the late 1990s.

2014

34 Sicard Street

A modern fraternity house built specifically for ZBT begins another period in Beta Delta’s history.

2017

45 Mine Street

The chapter moves to Mine Street and continues growing.

2020

41 Mine Street

The chapter grows significantly, setting new recruitment records in Fall 2024 and Fall 2025.

2025

Meissner Award

ZBT International recognizes Beta Delta as its Most Improved Chapter.

2026

106 College Avenue

ZBT letters return to College Avenue as Beta Delta comes back onto campus for the first time in ten years.
